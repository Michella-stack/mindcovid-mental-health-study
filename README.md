## About This Study

This repository contains the academic essay and supporting materials for a quantitative analysis of mental health outcomes among healthcare professionals managing COVID-19 outbreaks in Spain.

The study uses primary survey data from the **MINDCOVID project** (IMIM — Institut Hospital del Mar d'Investigacions Mèdiques, Barcelona) to explore anxiety, PTSD, panic attacks, and suicidal ideation in a sample of 9,138 healthcare workers across 19 Spanish hospitals during May 2020.

## Key Findings

- **~34%** of participants reported anxiety at higher than normal levels (variable C1_4)
- **~22%** showed signs of early PTSD (variables tx_12m, tx_lt)
- **~22%** had experienced at least one panic attack in the prior 30 days (variable C5)
- **>75%** gave no response on whether they felt they needed additional psychological support (D2_2)
- **~8%** reported suicidal ideation within the prior 30 days (variable C_15)

## Methods

- **Software:** STATA, Microsoft Excel
- **Analysis:** Descriptive statistics, multilevel regression, multivariate analysis
- **Scales:** GAD-7 (Generalised Anxiety Disorder), PTSD-4 screening scale
- **Data type:** Anonymous primary survey data (cohort observational model)
